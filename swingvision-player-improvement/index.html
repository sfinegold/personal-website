What I Want SwingVision To Do

Analyze my play, benchmark me, and tell me how to get better. Everything else is secondary.

  • 1

    Analyze My Sessions

    After every session, summarize what I did well and what I didn't. Actual insight, not just raw stats.

    • The richest analysis is locked behind successful auto-scoring. If the score isn't detected, I lose most of the session's value — even though the camera tracked every shot.
    • There's no real post-session summary pulling out key stats and takeaways. I have to dig through the data myself to figure out what mattered.
  • 2

    Benchmark Me

    Track my metrics over time and show me where I stand relative to NTRP/UTR levels. I want context for my numbers, not just the numbers.

  • 3

    Give Me an Improvement Plan

    Push me with specific next-session focus areas. Make the app feel like a coach, not a scoreboard.

  • 4

    Coaching on Autopilot

    I'd pay ~$30/month for a coach to review one session a week. Let me flag specific clips so they can review efficiently — coaching should run in the background, not be something I orchestrate.

Site Critique

A few high-impact opportunities.

Make the Home Tab About My Improvement

The home tab is currently an "other players" feed — random sessions from people I don't know, ranked by shot count. I have no reason to search for other players by name.

Opportunity: Push me content that helps me improve — players one level ahead of me, local opponents to scout for USTA leagues, AI highlight clips. Turn the feed into a competitive advantage.

Replace Rewards With Game Diagnosis

The referral rewards program occupies an entire primary nav tab for something I'd use once. That's prime real estate.

Opportunity: Replace it with something tied to improvement — a game diagnosis, level breakdown, or progress view that makes me open the app to check how I'm trending.

Desktop as Command Center

The desktop experience feels like an afterthought — swing.vision loads a marketing page rather than a dashboard. A lot of working professionals are on their computers all day and would love to review sessions and watch highlights on a big screen.

Opportunity: Make the website feel like a command center for your game. A logged-in desktop experience with stats, session film, and coaching reviews would be incredibly sticky for anyone who works at a computer.

Fix Recording Friction

Player identification should be automatic after months of tagged photos — this is a solved problem (Apple Photos, Google Photos). Partial sessions should still yield insights, and stats shouldn't be locked behind a broken auto-score gate.

Opportunity: Auto-identify me after 5+ sessions and decouple stats from scoring entirely. The data is captured — don't hide it.

Every Session Should Produce Insights

Right now, if the AI can't figure out the score, the session is treated like it barely happened. No shot breakdowns, no speed trends, no placement data — just a video with minimal stats. The camera still tracked every ball. The data is there.

Opportunity: Extract and surface stats and insights for every session regardless of whether scoring worked. Shot speed, placement, consistency, rally length — none of these depend on knowing the score. Make every recorded session valuable.
